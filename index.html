AUTOBIOGRAPHY

Blank Space Dreams: An Autobiography of Kindness, Quirks, and BTS Playlists I was born in a not-so-small estate on the outskirts of Nairobi, the second child in a family of five. My parents hail from the Luhya community of Busia County. They have a cute love story, but that's a tale for another day. My childhood memories are a bit hazy, but there are a few moments that stand out. I recall one day when I decided to wear my dad's shoes because I wanted to accompany him to work. He wouldn't buy me candy, so I thought it was a clever way to earn my own money. It didn't quite work out as planned, but it's a funny memory I cherish. Another vivid memory is the time my sister packed her bags to run away from home after being punished. She had received a thorough beating from our mom, and in my childlike innocence, I cried because I didn't want her to leave. Thankfully, she didn't go through with it, and our family remained intact. I was also a good child, respectful, and didn't need much supervision. The elders often remarked that I was mature for my age, with an old soul. Early school life was unremarkable. However, after high school, my life took a turn when I was diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder and chronic anxiety. Since then, I've been navigating life while masking these challenges. Living with autism spectrum disorder and chronic anxiety has its complexities. While I wouldn't say it's overwhelmingly difficult, there are certain day-to-day activities that trigger intense fear in me. For example, I find making calls and giving presentations particularly daunting. Though I might have used the phrase "deathly afraid," it's more of an exaggeration to convey the intensity of my feelings. I love the environment and nature, including the stars, the moon, lakes, waterfalls, and even the birds of the air. I consider caring for the environment to be my personal responsibility and do everything within my power to take care of Mother Earth, who is so nice to us. Occasionally, I go on marches and attend conferences to champion for environmental conservation and educate people on global warming and climate change. I resonate very much with the fig tree analogy by Sylvia Plath. I can't choose exactly what I want to be and what I want to do. I want to be an engineer, a poet, an artist, a doctor, and an ambassador. I want to study law, rocks, and psychology. I want to get married and be a nun and celibate. There's only so much one can do in one lifetime, but I dare to dream. If there's one thing I want to be remembered for, it's that I was a lover. A lover of the people around me, a lover of nature, a lover of literature, a lover of everything, and most importantly, that I loved myself enough to love everything and everyone else.
